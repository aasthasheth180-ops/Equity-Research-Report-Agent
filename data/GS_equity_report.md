## Risks

**Regulatory Capital Adequacy Risk**
Goldman Sachs, as a G-SIB, is subject to stringent risk-based regulatory capital and leverage requirements, largely based on Basel III. A failure to maintain sufficient capital levels, or an increase in required capital buffers under new regulations like the Basel III endgame, could restrict business activities, limit capital distributions, and negatively impact profitability. Severity: H, Probability: M. Mitigant: Proactive capital management strategies, including optimizing risk-weighted assets and maintaining a strong Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio, which was 12.5% at the end of Q1 2025, 110 basis points above the then-current requirement of 11.4%.

**Credit and Loan Loss Risk**
Goldman Sachs is exposed to credit risk through its lending activities, including corporate, real estate, and securities-based loans, which totaled $167.6 billion as of December 2025. A deterioration in economic conditions or specific industry downturns could lead to increased defaults and higher provisions for credit losses, impacting earnings. Severity: H, Probability: M. Mitigant: Diversified loan portfolio, rigorous credit underwriting standards, and ongoing monitoring of credit exposures, with a provision for credit losses of $315 million in Q1 2025 reflecting growth and impairments in the wholesale lending portfolio.

**Interest Rate Sensitivity Risk**
Changes in interest rates can significantly affect Goldman Sachs' net interest income, particularly within its transaction banking and lending activities. An unfavorable shift in the interest rate environment, such as rapid rate increases leading to higher funding costs or sustained low rates compressing lending margins, could reduce profitability. Severity: M, Probability: M. Mitigant: Active asset-liability management, including hedging strategies and dynamic pricing of deposits and loans, to mitigate the impact of interest rate fluctuations.

**Trading Revenue Volatility Risk**
Goldman Sachs' Global Banking & Markets segment, particularly FICC and Equities intermediation, is highly dependent on market activity levels and volatility. Unfavorable market conditions, such as reduced client activity, decreased volatility, or significant market dislocations, can lead to substantial declines in trading revenues. Severity: H, Probability: H. Mitigant: Diversification of trading activities across various asset classes and geographies, robust risk management frameworks, and a focus on client-driven intermediation rather than proprietary trading.

**Geopolitical and Macroeconomic Risk**
Global geopolitical instability, trade tensions, or a significant macroeconomic downturn can disrupt financial markets, reduce client confidence, and negatively impact all of Goldman Sachs' business segments. Such events can lead to reduced deal flow, lower asset valuations, and increased credit risk. Severity: H, Probability: H. Mitigant: Global diversification of operations, comprehensive scenario planning, and maintaining strong capital and liquidity positions to withstand adverse market shocks.

The single most material risk to our base case price target of $517.33 is the **Regulatory Capital Adequacy Risk**. This risk would be triggered if the final Basel III endgame capital requirements are significantly more stringent than currently anticipated, requiring Goldman Sachs to hold an additional 200 basis points or more in CET1 capital above our projections. Such an outcome would severely constrain the firm's ability to deploy capital for growth initiatives and shareholder returns, leading to a further reduction in our intrinsic valuation and representing a potential downside of approximately 15-20% to our price target.
